# Proportional challenge

### Q1 — Are we copying category bloat?

No. The recommendation excludes supplier portals, questionnaire authoring,
permission engines, integrations, document management and award optimisation.
It only completes the evaluation workflow the product already claims to show.

### Q2 — Is the proposed differentiator genuinely useful?

Plausibly, but not yet proven. Directing a panel to incomplete and divergent
items gives existing comparison and consensus logic a purpose. Validate the
workshop behaviour before claiming distinctiveness or adding advanced modes.

### Q3 — Does the bundle form one user outcome?

Yes. A panel can enter individual scores, see honest progress, identify the
items that need discussion and carry those items into reasoned consensus.

### Q4 — Are we ignoring or duplicating something already built?

The draft initially risked treating comparison, progress and moderation as new
features. They already exist. The MVP supplies live session input and connects
those surfaces rather than rebuilding them.

### Q5 — Is there a smaller serious MVP?

Four changes are the smallest serious workflow. Score entry alone would create
data without a product outcome; divergence without live inputs would remain a
demo; and moderation without a handoff would preserve the current navigation
gap.
