# Product-MVP research profile

Default profile for a one-person or very small build team making an
incremental product decision.

## Decision objective

Identify the best established and emerging product capabilities, map them
against the current MVP, and choose the smallest coherent next increment that
improves usefulness and differentiation.

## Hard research ceilings

- Maximum **5 commercial products per scout**.
- Maximum **5 useful sources per scout**.
- A product page, documentation page, public demo/video, screenshot set, or
  review page counts as one source.
- Stop earlier when additional searching repeats existing patterns.
- Do not silently exceed either ceiling. If coverage is insufficient, record
  the precise gap and propose a follow-on mission for the repo owner to
  approve.

These are scope controls, not quality targets. Five weak sources do not become
good research by filling the quota.

## Lightweight provenance

For every retained observation record:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Product | Commercial product observed |
| Capability or pattern | Normalised feature/pattern name |
| Layer | Backend/workflow, UX, or distinctive |
| Observation | What is visibly shipped, documented, or demonstrated |
| Source | Link/path and optional screenshot reference |
| Signal | Common, emerging, isolated, or uncertain |
| Limitation | Marketing-only, inaccessible detail, unclear value, etc. |

No Evidence/Inference/Hypothesis grammar or independent verification is
required by default. Links exist for traceability and visual follow-up, not to
turn product discovery into academic citation work.

Signal vocabulary within the bounded scan:

- **Common** — observed in at least 3 of the products inspected by the
  relevant scout.
- **Emerging** — observed in 2 products.
- **Isolated** — observed in 1 product; may still be a differentiator.
- **Uncertain** — public material does not establish whether it is shipped.

## Required convergence

The Lead and synthesist must reduce the combined scan to:

### A. Category/backend capability shortlist — maximum 5

For each: category signal, user problem, current-product state, smallest
meaningful delta, and value.

### B. Workflow/UX shortlist — maximum 5

For each: observed products, user problem, current-product state,
applicability, and smallest meaningful delta.

### C. Distinctive-feature shortlist — maximum 3

For each: why uncommon, user value, product fit, risk, and validation need.

### D. Current-product delta

Classify every shortlisted candidate as:

- already strong;
- present but weak;
- missing;
- deliberately excluded;
- poor fit.

### E. Recommended next MVP

One coherent bundle of **3–5 changes** which produces a named user outcome.
Also provide:

- one reserve MVP;
- three things not to build now;
- the uncertainties that must be validated before implementation;
- a suggested incremental sequence, not a detailed build plan.

## Selection lenses

Use High/Medium/Low judgements, not faux-precise aggregate maths:

- user value;
- category signal;
- current-product gap or existing leverage;
- fit with product principles;
- differentiation;
- achievable MVP scope for a one-person team;
- validation uncertainty.

Frequency informs priority but does not decide it. An isolated feature may win
as a differentiator; a common feature may be enterprise bloat or already
adequately present.

## Proportional challenge gate

No formal verifier or full contrarian phase by default. Before finalising, ask
at most five questions:

1. Are we copying category bloat?
2. Is the proposed differentiator genuinely useful?
3. Does the bundle form one user outcome?
4. Are we ignoring or duplicating something already built?
5. Is there a smaller serious MVP?

Invoke the evidence verifier only when a finalist depends on a consequential
claim that is inaccessible, contradictory, regulatory, externally publishable,
or expensive to act on. Verify that claim only, not the full corpus.

## Definition of done

The repo owner can approve, amend, reserve or reject the proposed next MVP in
one sitting. The brief is incomplete if it merely reports the market or leaves
an unranked backlog.
