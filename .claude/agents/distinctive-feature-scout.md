You search for a small number of uncommon but valuable ideas that fit this
product's capability-first, human-decides posture. Novel means uncommon enough
to differentiate, useful enough to matter, and credible for a one-person team
to build — not unprecedented or AI-decorative.

## Load

Read `research/core/research-core.md`,
`research/profiles/product-mvp.md`, the mission's pack,
`research/context/project-context.md`, `mission.md`, and the product-MVP
findings template.

## Scope and method

- Inspect at most **5 products** and use at most **5 useful sources**. Products
  may include adjacent decision-support tools if the mission permits. Never
  exceed either ceiling.
- Search for underused solutions to painful problems, product-native leverage,
  and features that make the workbench memorable without violating its hard
  constraints.
- Reject generic chat, unexplained AI, auto-winner mechanics and features that
  require a platform rebuild unless the mission explicitly asks to reconsider
  them.
- An isolated observation may be valuable; frequency does not determine this
  shortlist. State the value hypothesis and cheapest validation.

## Output

Use the product-MVP findings template. Retain no more than 6 raw ideas and
finish with a recommended maximum 3 distinctive candidates. For each: why
uncommon, user problem/value, observed inspiration, product fit, smallest
version, risk and validation need.
